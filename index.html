Our Team

John Fitzgerald

John is a partner of Lennon Fitzgerald and is widely recognised as a leading expert in his field.

John graduated from Trinity College in 2003 with an Business and Economics Law degree and was admitted as a Chartered Accountant to Chartered Accountants Ireland in 2007 having being placed in the 1st percentile in the Institute's final admitting examinations. John also holds an Advanced Diploma in Employment Law from Kings Inns, is a qualified Pension Trustee and is a Qualified Financial Advisor (QFA).

Before founding Lennon Fitzgerald, John worked as a Accountant for the "Big 4" firm KPMG for a number of years before spending time working overseas working and directly managing not-for-profit organisations on the front line for not-for-profit organisations and organisations in the public sector.

John is a leading expert in the provision of Accounting, HR and related Advisory Services for not-for-profit organisations and has advised organisations on expansions, restructuring, strategy, donor relations and fundraising strategies.

Outside of Lennon Fitzgerald, John has experience as a director on a number of Boards.

Luke Lennon

Luke is a partner of Lennon Fitzgerald and is widely recognised as a not-for-profit leading practioner.

Luke graduated from Limerick University in 2003 with an Business Studies Law degree and was admitted as a Certified Public Accountant to Certified Public Accountants Ireland in 2007 having being sailed through the Institute's final admitting examinations on his first attempt. Luke also is Certified from the Association of Certified Fraud in the USA and Before founding Lennon Fitzgerald, Luke worked as a Director of Finance in the commerical sectors for a number of years before changing career paths and working overseas for GOAL. John is a leading expert in the provision of Accounting and related Advisory Services for not-for-profit organisations and has advised organisations on best in practice systems, forensic reviews, statutory compliance and business plans.
